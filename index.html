The Great Blue Turaco

photograph by Michael Gwyther-Jones

HABITAT

The great blue turaco lives in the tropical forests of West Africa. It glides and hops from tree to tree. It lives in the canopy.

TRAITS

The birds are 70-75cm's long, and the females tend to be larger than the males. The Turaco has a blue body with a yellow bellow, black and yellow tail feathers, a yellow beak and black feathers sticking up on top of its head.

NESTING

The Great Blue Turaco's nests are between 8-25 meters (25-75 feet) off the ground, often above water. The female will lay pale blue eggs in the nest. Their eggs hatch in 29-31 days.

STATUS

In Western Africa, the great blue turaco is hunted for consumption (to be eaten), and also trapped for trade in some parts. The speciies is not globally threatened, nor endangered.

DIET

The great blue heron eats many different things: fruits, buds, shoots, leaves, flowers and some insects.

FUN FACTS

  • The Great Blue Turaco is a poor flyer, but a great climber.
  • They often travel in groups (or flocks)
  • You can see a Great Blue Turaco at The Bronx Zoo!
