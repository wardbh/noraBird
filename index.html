The Great Blue Turaco

photograph by Michael Gwyther-Jones

HABITAT

The great blue turaco lives in the tropical forests of West Africa. It glides and hops from tree to tree. It lives in the canopy.

TRAITS

The birds are 70-75cm's long, and the females tend to be larger than the males.

NESTING

The Great Blue Turaco's nests are between 8-25 meters (25-75 feet) off the ground, often above water. The female will lay two pale blue eggs in the nest. Their eggs hatch in 29-31 days.
